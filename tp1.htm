by ijuxda » Fri Dec 24, 2010 9:34 pm

sabibaeurolite wrote:i had a quick question about transmission and how it connects when you set a proxy

i have selected to connect to trackers through a proxy however even when i have this option checked and the proxy set to XXX.XX.XX.XXX it continues to download i would suppose from my actual ip

i was just wondering if someone could explain to me how transmission downloads when a proxy is checked and how if possible to disallow automatically reverting back to downloading from my ip

The proxy you have set for trackers is used for all URLs supported by the curl library, not just tracker communication. So it should proxy torrent file downloads too (i.e. from "File/Add URL" or transmission-remote -a). You can check that your proxy is being used by either setting it to an address that you know has no proxy (e.g. 127.0.0.1 port 12345 or whatever port is not being used on your machine) and seeing what happens. You can also use a service like checkmytorrentip.com to verify a working tracker proxy is hiding your address.

If you confirm the above and still think it is "reverting" or something else is wrong, you can try my transmission branch (report issues here) which I specifically maintain to have at least the tracker proxy capability. Unfortunately this is only if you are on linux, I cannot directly help you if you are on macosx.

Also, until the transmission developers feel they can trust their userbase enough to divulge the real reasons for why proxy support was removed, feel free to complain here and here.

Being a free software project maintainer demands a certain level of transparency, accountability and respect. All the more so when the project provides binary packages and accepts donations to fund its operations.

Above all, nobody, no matter how far invested into the project, is beyond reproach for mistakes, misrepresentation, or abuses of status and authority.

As should be made clear to everyone following this situation, by now the central issue is not whether or how proxy support should be implemented in the transmission codebase, but the fundamental lack of professionalism and undeserved contempt that discussions regarding this feature have brought to light.

In particular it is important to keep in mind the following questions when judging the arguments and exchanges taking place in this thread, this one, and in these trac tickets #3688 #3817:

  1. Why was the removal of the proxy feature not included in the release notes for version 2.12?
  2. Why was a another ticket started (#3817) when the feedback in #3688 was clearly not in favor of the changes?
  3. Why have the changes in #3817 been pushed through into the public svn repository (r11512, r11514, r11515, r11520) despite the ticket not being closed and agreement in the discussion not being reached?
  4. Who is Jordan? And if Jordan is an alias, why was this alias created?
If a public answer to any of these questions is not provided by a trustworthy representative of the transmission project, how can it be expected that users, contributors and third-pary onlookers will not express uncertainty and doubt about the direction and control of past, present and future development?

What prevents someone from speculating about the true causes of these occurences: "Has the transmission project been compromised by legal action from more powerful entities, so much so that developers must remove features and make progressively more ludicrous attempts at covering up the influence?"

I reiterate that the above is purely speculation and the result of the above enumerated questions remaining unanswered. It is representative though, of the private inquiries that I have received regarding this matter.

I admit to everyone that what is happening here is a lot to process. I myself am unsure of the best way to proceed, but I will always endeavour to keep the best interests of all parties at heart. Slander notwithstanding, I do want transmission to be the best bittorrent client, and a fair and respected Free Software project.

So please, if you care about this issue, make your opinion publicly known. Discuss it with others who you think would be interested or affected by the consequences. Until a satisfactory resolution is achieved do not be misled by tangential arguments, and do not forget the original premise of the debate.
